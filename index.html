Vice President | President Elect

   Born in China and raised in New York, Qianyu Wu is the child of immigrant parents and a lower middle class family. Her upbringing has greatly influenced her interest in politics and zeal to assist those of a similar background. Qianyu Wu is the treasurer for Science Olympiad and a member of Speech and Debate Team at Stuyvesant. As the treasurer for Science Olympiad, she has had the responsibility of budget planning and regulating the financial expenses for the club. The Speech and Debate Team has allowed Qianyu to be personally connected to the controversial issues she speaks out against and become an advocate for the rights of people with different backgrounds. Through her internship at the Queens Supreme Court, Qianyu had firsthand experience of observing unfair court trials, the lack of assistance and care for the elderly and disabled, and forceful removal of illegal immigrants from the court. The exposure to these problems has only deepened her passion and commitment to improving the country by establishing equal opportunity. Dedicated to providing a brighter future for Americans, Qianyu hopes to ensure a government that is not only impartial and proactive but also one that takes care of its people.

Domestic Issues

__________________

  • Education Reform

    Students often complain that they take classes that aren't relevant and learn things that they'll never use again. We want to improve the quality of schools by improvising more relevant courses, with the option to take more advanced classes. Education from K-11th grade essentially lead up to the college process, and we understand that it is a stressful one. That's why we aim to waive college application fees, and have schools provide more resources that will aid students in the process. Furthermore, U.S. schools will provide improved mental health care and guidance services for all students to help relieve stress and ultimately reduce suicide rates.

  • Jobs

    We want to create a federal program that would allow for more internship opportunities for high schoolers to explore various fields. This will help students gain work experience, develop and find their career interests, and exercise social networking skills that many students lack. President FDR’s New Deal created jobs for young Americans to prevent the rise of crime rates. We can do the same, but better. Following the precedent President FDR laid, we can revise and create a new plan to create jobs for those who are unemployed and in school. Furthermore we will enforce minimum wage. A majority of those workers are immigrants, illegal and legal. Our goal will be to enforce the minimum wage so that more people in the U.S.

  • Gender Equality

    We will fully support LGBTQ rights. It is a constitutional right for people to identify and express themselves as who they believe to be. No laws should limit these personal freedoms of the person. We will strive for equal wages regardless of race or gender, but rather by skill.

  • Gun Control

    In recent years, so many school and public shootings have been brought to national attention. This should not be the case. We will strengthen background checks and mental health screenings for any looking to purchase or own a gun. The right to bear arms is a constitutional right, but we cannot and should not allow those who have poor mental health or a history of violence to bear such arms.

  • Immigration

    Dreamers will be provided with shelter, education, and equal protections under the law while in the country. We aim to have a looser immigration policy, but with a quota. Those seeking and requiring asylum will be granted it.

Foreign Issues

__________________

Latin America

The United States will approach Latin American nations with soft power. We will be trading partners, extending helping hands if needed and we will not tangle with their domestic issues. We will impose immigration quotas. The same amount of immigrants will be restricted for every country. We need to make sure people in our country are taken care of first, namely the homeless, jobless, and those in poverty.

China

The United States will try to coexist with China as global superpowers to prevent further tensions with China and avoid the Thucydides's Trap. However, China needs a more open economic policy. The Chinese government needs to minimize subsidizing their state-owned companies to give equal opportunities for every company across the world to gain contracts. Business espionage and violation of U.S. sanctions must be stopped. In order to maintain friendly relations with both China and Taiwan, we aim to try policies similar to the Ping-Pong Diplomacy during the Nixon administration. We will try to slowly persuade China to allow Taiwanese people to choose whether or not they want to be a part of China.

North Korea

We recognize North Korea's nuclear capabilities as a threat. We will tighten our sanctions and try to pressure other nations to cut ties with North Korea until they denuclearize. We will end summits and meetings that just lead to nowhere, and will work tightly with China, South Korea, and Russia to take firm action.
